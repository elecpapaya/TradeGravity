Definitions at a glance

What this view means—and does not mean

Total trade

Reporter exports plus reporter imports with the selected partner.

Exports / imports

Flows are recorded from each reporter country's perspective, with USA or China as partner.

USA / China share

Each anchor divided by USA plus China in this two-anchor view. Neither is a whole-world market share.

Exposure balance

USA share minus China share. Positive means US-leaning; negative means China-leaning within the two anchors.

Position shift

Current balance minus the previous comparable balance. Its sign shows movement toward USA or China, not causality.

Dual exposure

Twice the smaller anchor share. It describes how material both anchors are, not whole-world dependency.

Two-partner HHI

Concentration across the two published anchors only. It is not whole-world supplier concentration.

Latest by reporter

Uses each reporter's latest published observations. Select an exact period for cross-country comparisons.

Growth

Change from the published previous period shown for that observation; missing baselines remain unavailable.

Normalized values

Per-capita and GDP-share views combine trade with the published World Bank country context and its own year.

Headlines

Recent GDELT headlines are optional context. Their rolling window is not aligned to historical trade periods.
